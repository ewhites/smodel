Standard Model Nomenclature
  1. Fermion - A family of fundamental particals in the standard model with a spin number of 1/2, 3/2, etc. Fermions are the particles
    that make up the matter in the universe.
  2. Boson - A family of fundamental particals with spin numbers 0, 1, 2, etc. Bosons are the particals that carry out the interations of the fundemental forces.
  3. Spin - A quanty that every fundemtal parical is observed to have. Spin has the same units as clasical angular momentum; however, the partcals to not actually spin in the classical sense.
  4. Vector - A type of quantity that is described by both magnitude (size or strength) and direction.
  5. Scalar - A type of quantity that is described by only magnitude.
  6. Tensor - Mathematical concept that generalizes a vector. Similar to a vector that can point in all directions.
  7. Vector Field - Some region that is under the influnce of a vector quantity.
  8. Scalar Field - Some region that is under the influnce of a scalar quantity.
  9. Tensor Field - Some region that is under the influnce of a tensor quantity.
  10. Angular Momentum - Momentum is the product of the mass and velocity, Angular momentum is the rotational anolog of momentum.
  11. Quantized - A distribution that can only be divided into discrete values or steps
  12. Pauli Exclusion Principle - A principal introduced by Austrian physicist Wolfgang Pauli that states no two fermions can exist in the same space at the same time
  13. Matter - A substance that has mass and ocupies space.
  14. Force - A vector quantity that can influence the motion of objects
  15. Quarks - The heavier set of six fermions that must bind together to exist.
  16. Leptons - The lighter set of six fermions that can exist indepentdently.
  17. Baryons - A set of three Quarks bound together.
  18. Mesons - A set of two Quarks bound together.
  19. Hadrons - Any set of Quarks bound together by the nuclear strong force.
  20. Proton - A common Baryon made of two up quarks and one down quark. Protons are one of two particles in the nucleus of the atom.
  21. Neutron - A common Baryon made of two down quarks and one up quark. Neutrons are one of two particles in the nucleus of the atom.
  22. Neutrino - A subcatagory of 3 leptons that are extremly small in mass and nearly impossible to interact with.
  23. Nucleons - A particle in that exists in the nucleus of an atom
  24. Generations - Generations are a 3 divisions of fermions that each contain 2 quarks and two leptons. The generations have different quantum spin numbers and mass but have the same electric and strong interactions.
  25. Electro-magnetic force - One of the four fundemental forces. The interations of the Electromagnetic force are carried out by the photon.
  26. Gravity - One of the four fundemental forces. The force of gravity has no known boson that carries out it's interactions.
  27. Weak nuclear force - One of the four fundemental forces. The weak nuclear force is carried out by the W and Z bosons
  28. Strong nuclear force - One of the four fundemental forces. The strong nuclear force is carried out by the gluon.
  29. Spin number - Spin number is a quantum number to desrcibe the spin angular momentum of a particle.
  30. Hyperons - A Baryon that contains strange quarks but no charm, bottom or top quark.
